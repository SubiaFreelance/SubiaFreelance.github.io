Career intake.

This is the long version of the contact form. It's for clients who don't have an existing resume — or who have one that doesn't represent their work anymore. The answers below are the raw material I'll use to build your CV.

Before you start. This form takes longer than most. Plan on 30–45 minutes. Don't worry about polished writing — this is raw material for me to shape, not the final product. Typos, fragments, parentheticals, half-finished thoughts — all fine. The depth of what you put in is the depth of what I have to work with.
Section 1
About you
The basics. Identity and contact info.
Section 2
Where you're headed
The strategic context. What you're trying to land, and what the audience for your CV cares about.
Section 3
Career history
Your roles, structured the way professional CV writers think about them. Use as many of the five slots as apply, starting with most recent. Older roles can be combined in the "Anything else" section at the bottom.

More than five roles? Combine the older ones into a brief paragraph in Section 7's "anything else" field — recent and relevant matters more than complete.

Section 4
Signature work
The two or three things across your career you're most proud of. These often cross multiple jobs and don't fit neatly into bullet points.
Section 5
Skills, education, and credentials
The qualifications layer. Be inclusive — easier for me to cut than to invent.
Section 6
Voice and value proposition
The part that turns a structurally correct CV into a Beacon-quality one. Your voice, your through-line, the things that aren't on any resume.
Section 7
Logistics and target material
Last section. Anything that didn't fit, plus material that helps me match the CV to the actual market.
